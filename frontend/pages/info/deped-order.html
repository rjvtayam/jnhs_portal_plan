DepEd Order No. 8, s. 2015

Policy Guidelines on Classroom Assessment for the K to 12 Basic Education Program

Overview

DepEd Order No. 8, s. 2015, also known as the "Policy Guidelines on Classroom Assessment for the K to 12 Basic Education Program," establishes the assessment standards and grading system used in all public schools in the Philippines under the K-12 curriculum.

This order defines how student learning is measured across Written Works, Performance Tasks, and Quarterly Assessments, and provides the transmutation table used to convert raw scores into final grades.

Grading System

Component Weights

The quarterly grade is computed from two components:

Component Percentage Description
Written Works (WW) 60% Quizzes, seatworks, homeworks, and long tests
Performance Tasks (PT) 40% Projects, performances, and other activities

Quarterly Grading Formula

The quarterly grade is computed as:

Quarter Formula
Quarter 1 Initial Grade = (WW * 0.60) + (PT * 0.40)
Quarter 2 Initial Grade = (WW * 0.60) + (PT * 0.40)
Quarter 3 Initial Grade = (WW * 0.60) + (PT * 0.40)
Quarter 4 Initial Grade = (WW * 0.60) + (PT * 0.40)

Transmutation Formula

Transmuted Grade = 50 + (Raw Score / 2)

This formula converts the initial grade into the final transmuted grade displayed on the report card.

Passing Grade

The passing grade for all subjects in the K-12 Basic Education Program is 75 (Seventy-Five). Any grade below 75 is considered failing.

Assessment Types

Written Works

Includes all forms of written assessments such as quizzes, seatworks, homework, assignments, and long tests. These measure the learner's knowledge and understanding of concepts and skills.

Performance Tasks

Includes projects, performances, portfolios, demonstrations, and other authentic assessments. These measure the learner's ability to apply knowledge and skills in real-world situations.
